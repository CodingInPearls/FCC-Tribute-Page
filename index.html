Admiral William H. McRaven

Watch 2014 Commencement Address

Here's a timeline to McRaven's Life & Achievements:

    Personal Life

  • November 6, 1955: Born in Pinehurst, North Carolina. Parents are Anna Elizabeth (Long) and Colonel Claude C. McRaven.
  • His family moved to San Antonio, Texas during his Elementary school years.
  • Attended Theodore Roosevelt High School, Texas. He was also part of the track team.
  • 1977: Graduated from the University of Texas at Austin with a Bachelor's degree in journalism. He was part of the track team, and a member of the Naval Reserve Officers Training Corps.
  • 1978: Married a fellow undergraduate from the University of Texas at Austin, Georgeann Brady. They have 3 children.

    • Career Life

  • Holds a Master's degree from Naval Postgraduate School. He helped establish and was the first one to graduate from such Special Operations/Low Intensity Conflict curriculum.
  • Attended Basic Underwater Demolition/SEAL training right after graduating at University of Texas at Austin.
  • January 1978: Graduated SEAL training after six months.
  • 1979 & 1981: Deployed to the Phillippines with Naval Special Warfare Unit One. Entitled to wearing the Special Warfare Insignia.
  • 1983: At 27 years old, McRaven was fired by Richard Marcinkoand transferred to another east coast SEAL team.
  • McRaven has served many command assignments within Special Ops. Including Squadron Commander, Executive Officer, Task Unit Commander during the Persian Gulf War, Commanding Officer of SEAL Team THREE from 1994 to 1996 and Commanding Officer of Naval Special Warfare Group ONE from 1999 to 2001. Just to name a few.
  • 1993: Earned Master of Arts degree at Naval Postgraduate School. His Master's thesis has been republished as Special Ops: Case Studies in Special Operations Warfare: Theory and Practice.
  • Leader of a group that targeted Al Qaeda in Iraq, 'Task Force 714'.
  • April 6 2011: Nominated by President Barack Obama for promotion from rank of vice admiral to admiral and was appointed as the ninth commander of USSOCOM.
  • Operation Neptune's Spear special ops led, organized and overseen by McRaven that led to the killing of Osama bin Laden. McRaven built on stragety to counter-terrorism since 2001.
  • December 2011: McRaven was runner-up for Time's Person of the Year for his role in Operation Neptune's Spear.

    • Military Retirement

  • June 2014: After 37 years, McRaven's request for retirement was approved.
  • September 1 2014: Admiral McRaven retired from the U.S. Navy. Retiring as 'Bull Frog', he was the longest serving Navy SEAL still on duty.

    • Life after the Navy

  • January 5 2015: McRaven was selected as the next Chancellor of the University of Texas System. McRaven spent $215M dollars on an expansion for the University of Texas.
  • May 31 2018: Stepped away from his position as the Chancellor of the University due to chronic health issues and a desire to spend more time with his family.
